Our Story

Levantina was founded by Rana Al-Nahhas, a Syrian-American woman with roots in the city of Homs and a love for the vibrant food traditions of the Levant. Living now in Washington, DC, Rana created Levantina during the holy month of Ramadan as a way to share the flavor, texture, and meaning of a dessert that transcends borders: knafeh.

At Levantina, knafeh is more than a treat—it’s a story. Made with a blend of melted cheese and delicious "ashta" cream, our knafeh is prepared "khishni," giving it that perfect golden crunch. It’s the kind of dessert that sparks memories of home, connects generations, and brings people together.

Knafeh also carries weight. With historical roots in the Levant, it evokes a broader spirit of pan-Arabism, cultural pride, and the ongoing fight for justice in Palestine and beyond. Through Levantina, Rana offers a slice of resistance as much as sweetness—an edible expression of joy, memory, and liberation.

You’ll find Levantina’s knafeh at pop-ups, events, and community tables across DC. Every bite is handmade, heartfelt, and rooted in a legacy of resilience.
